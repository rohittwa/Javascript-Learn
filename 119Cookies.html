Cookies

Cookies are exposed as the cookie object of the Document object. This property is both readable and writable.
When you assign a string to document.cookie, the browser parses it as a cookie and adds it to its list of cookies. There are several parts to each cookies, many of them optional.
Syntax:-
document.cookie = "name=value";
document.cookie = "name=value; expires=date; domain=domain; path=path; secure";
document.cookie = "name=value; max-age=inSeconds; domain=domain; path=path; secure";

Ex:-
document.cookie = "username=geeky"; document.cookie = "username=geeky; expires=Monday, 3-Sep-2022 09:00:00 UTC";

Note: name-value pair must not contain any white-space character, commas or semicolons.
